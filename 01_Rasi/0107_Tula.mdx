**Tula Rashi**, the seventh sign in the [Vedic zodiac system](../index.mdx) corresponding to Libra, represents the pinnacle of harmony, justice, and aesthetic balance. As detailed in classical texts like the Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS), Tula embodies the scales of justice, serving as the natural lord of the seventh house (kalatra bhava) governing marriage, partnerships, and public dealings. Ruled by **[Venus (Shukra)](../03_Planets/0306_Venus.mdx)** in its mooltrikona sign and exalted in **[Saturn (Shani)](../03_Planets/0307_Saturn.mdx)**, Tula natives navigate life through relational intelligence and diplomatic wisdom in [Vedic Astrology](../index.mdx).

The sign's air element and cardinal quality create a dynamic tension between social harmony and decisive action, as elaborated in texts like the Saravali and Phaladeepika. This comprehensive guide explores Tula's technical foundations, planetary dynamics, house placements, and remedial measures for cultivating justice and balanced relationships.

## Technical Profile of Tula Rashi

Tula Rashi occupies the 180-degree arc from 180° to 210° in the sidereal zodiac, characterized by its cardinal (chara) quality and masculine polarity. According to classical Vedic astrology:

- **Element**: Vayu Tattva (Air) - Governs communication, intellect, and social dynamics
- **Quality**: Cardinal (Chara) - Initiates social change through diplomatic action
- **Polarity**: Masculine - Active, expressive, and outwardly focused
- **Guna**: Rajas - Dynamic, action-oriented, ambitious
- **Ruling Planet**: Venus (Shukra) - Mooltrikona sign, governing relationships and aesthetics
- **Exaltation**: Saturn (Shani) - Maximum dignity at 20° Tula, enhancing discipline and justice
- **Debilitation**: [Sun (Surya)](../03_Planets/0301_Sun.mdx) - Falls at 10° Tula, weakening leadership and self-expression
- **Natural House Lordship**: Seventh house (kalatra bhava) - Marriage, business partnerships, public enemies
- **Direction**: West - Associated with sunset, completion, and social harmony
- **Color**: Blue (indigo) - Venusian symbolism of peace, trust, and aesthetic beauty
- **Gemstone**: Diamond (Heera) - Venusian gemstone for relational harmony and prosperity
- **Lucky Number**: 6 - Venus number, representing balance and partnership
- **Deity**: Lakshmi (Sri) - Goddess of wealth, beauty, and harmonious abundance
- **Yoni**: Vanara (monkey) - Intelligent and adaptable social creature
- **Neutral**: Vrishchika, Kanya - Water and earth signs offering complementary balance

### [Saturn (Shani): Exalted Justice](../03_Planets/0307_Saturn.mdx)
Tula is the sign of **exaltation (Ucha)** for Saturn. The planet of discipline and karma reaches its highest dignity at 20 degrees of Libra. This signifies that structural integrity and long-term justice (Saturn) reach their most refined and fair expression when operating through the objective and social environment of Tula.

### [The Sun (Surya): Peak Debilitation (Neecha)](../03_Planets/0301_Sun.mdx)
Conversely, the Sun reaches its lowest dignity (**debilitation**) at 10 degrees of Libra. This indicates that the individual ego and authoritative power (Sun) must be surrendered in order to achieve the social harmony and collective balance that Tula demands.

## Classical References in Shastras

Tula Rashi holds significant importance in Vedic astrological literature as the sign of cosmic balance:

**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS)**: Describes Tula as the "cosmic scales of justice" where Venus finds its natural expression. Parasara notes that Tula natives possess "the wisdom of equilibrium and the charm of divine beauty" - combining diplomatic intelligence with aesthetic sensibility.

**Saravali**: Emphasizes Tula's role as the relationship cornerstone, stating that "just as scales balance weights, so do Tula natives balance human relationships with fairness and grace."

**Jataka Parijata**: Details Tula's diplomatic nature, noting that Saturn's exaltation creates "divine justice combined with patient wisdom," where leadership serves the greater social good.

**Brihat Jataka**: Varahamihira describes Tula as the sign of "aesthetic harmony and relational intelligence," where Venus's rulership manifests as both material beauty and spiritual balance.

These classical references establish Tula as the cornerstone of relational astrology in Vedic tradition.
