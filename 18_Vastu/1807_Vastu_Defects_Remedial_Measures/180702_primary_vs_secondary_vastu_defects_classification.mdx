## What are Primary and Secondary Vastu Defects? {#what-are-primary-and-secondary-vastu-defects}

**Primary Vastu Defects** are major structural flaws that directly contradict the fundamental principles of Vastu Shastra. These defects have a strong and immediate impact on the occupants' well-being.

**Secondary Vastu Defects** are minor flaws that are less severe than primary defects. While they can still cause problems, their impact is generally less pronounced.

- **Astrological significance**: Primary defects can significantly amplify negative planetary influences, while secondary defects have a more subtle effect.
- **Vastu applications**: Prioritizing the correction of primary defects is essential for restoring balance and harmony to a home.
- **Spiritual dimensions**: Addressing Vastu defects, both primary and secondary, is a way of showing respect for the natural laws of the universe.

### Historical Context

The classification of Vastu defects is based on the ancient scriptures of Vastu Shastra, which provide a detailed understanding of the impact of different architectural features on the energy of a building.

### Vastu Shastra Applications

Vastu Shastra provides a clear framework for classifying and prioritizing the correction of Vastu defects.

- **Directional alignment**: The location of a defect is a key factor in determining its severity. Defects in the northeast, for example, are considered more serious than defects in other directions.
- **Energy flow**: Primary defects are those that severely obstruct the flow of prana, while secondary defects cause minor disruptions.
- **The Five Elements**: The classification of a defect also depends on which of the five elements it affects.

## How to Classify Vastu Defects {#how-to-classify-vastu-defects}

### Step 1: Identify the Defect

The first step is to identify all the Vastu defects in a building. This can be done through a combination of self-assessment and professional consultation.

### Step 2: Determine the Severity

Once the defects have been identified, they need to be classified as primary or secondary based on their severity.

- **Primary Defects**: Main entrance in the south or southwest, toilet in the northeast, kitchen in the north, master bedroom in the southeast.
- **Secondary Defects**: Improper placement of furniture, use of inauspicious colors, clutter in the home.

### Step 3: Prioritize the Corrections

After classifying the defects, the corrections should be prioritized based on their severity. Primary defects should always be addressed first.
