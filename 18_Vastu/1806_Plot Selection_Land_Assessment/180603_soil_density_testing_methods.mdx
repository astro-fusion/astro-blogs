## Calculation and Analysis Methods {#calculation}

### Step-by-Step Calculation Process

**Required Information**:
- Plot location and intended construction type
- Clean, undisturbed ground area for testing

**Calculation Formula**:
As per Vastu tradition, the test is observational:

1. **Prepare the Ground**: Select a clean spot on the site.
2. **Dig the Pit**:
   - Depth: One hand span (~15-20 cm)
   - Width & Length: Each one hand span
3. **Excavate and Set Aside Soil**: Carefully remove all soil from the pit, keeping it aside without losing any material.
4. **Refill the Pit**: Try to refill the pit with the same excavated soil.
5. **Interpret the Results**:
   - **Soil remains after filling**: Indicates *good density*; site is excellent for construction.
   - **Pit exactly filled**: Indicates *medium quality*; acceptable for construction with standard precautions.
   - **Pit remains unfilled**: Indicates *poor density*; soil is loose, not fit for supporting major structures without significant reinforcement.

**Interpretation Logic**:  
- Soil exceeding the pit demonstrates it was packed densely, minimizing voids and maximizing support.  
- Soil just filling the pit is standard—neither excessive compaction nor looseness.  
- Less soil filling the pit signals high porosity and potential instability[6][8].
