## Calculation and Analysis Methods {#calculation}

### Step-by-Step Calculation Process

**Required Information**:
- Exact plot dimensions (length and width)
- Plot orientation relative to cardinal directions
- Any irregularities in shape or angles

**Calculation Formula**:
1. **Length-to-Width Ratio**: Divide the length by the width. Ideal ratio is 1:1 (square) or up to 1:2 (rectangle).
2. **Angular Assessment**: Measure all corner angles. Ideal corners should be 90° (square angles).
3. **Directional Alignment**: Determine how the plot aligns with cardinal directions.
4. **Slope Evaluation**: Measure the natural slope of the land. Ideally, the southwest and south should be higher than the northeast.

When dealing with non-ideal plot shapes, consider these Vastu remedies:

- Construct a boundary wall around a rectangular portion of the land

**For L-Shaped Plots**:
- If possible, purchase additional land to fill the missing corner

**For Plots with Extended Corners**:
- Extensions on the northeast side are considered auspicious and bring wealth and happiness
- Extensions on other sides, particularly southwest, should be avoided or remediated
- Consider gifting or not utilizing portions that extend beyond an ideal rectangular shape

## Common Mistakes to Avoid {#common-mistakes}

### Error #1: Ignoring Corner Angles
Many people focus only on plot size while overlooking the importance of right angles (90°). Vastu principles emphasize that all four corners should ideally be at 90° angles, with special attention to the northeast corner.

**Solution**: If corners aren't at right angles, consider creating boundaries within the plot that form proper 90° angles for the main structure.

### Error #2: Selecting Plots with Inappropriate Slopes
A common mistake is purchasing land with slopes that contradict Vastu principles. According to Vastu, the southwest and west should be higher than the northeast and east.

**Solution**: Perform proper leveling work before construction or implement landscaping solutions to create the appropriate slope.

### Error #3: Choosing Plots with Unfavorable Surroundings
Many buyers focus exclusively on the plot itself while ignoring surrounding elements that affect energy flow:

**Avoid plots near**:
- Graveyards, cemeteries, or tombs
- Foul-smelling areas (within 80-100 feet)
- Dead-end roads pointing directly at the plot
- Hills or mountains in the east or north sides

**Favor plots with**:
- Water bodies (rivers, canals) in the north or east
- Hills or elevated land in the south or west
- Open spaces in the north and east

**Q2: Can irregular-shaped plots be made Vastu-compliant?**

A: Yes, irregular plots can be made more Vastu-compliant through various remedial measures. These include creating rectangular boundaries within the irregular shape, using landscaping to balance energies, proper placement of rooms and functions, and implementing specific Vastu corrections based on the particular irregularity.

**Q3: How does plot orientation affect Vastu compliance?**

A: Plot orientation is crucial in Vastu Shastra. Plots facing north or east are generally considered more auspicious than those facing south or west. The ideal orientation allows morning sunlight to enter the home, promoting positive energy and health benefits. Cardinal direction alignment also determines the proper placement of rooms and functions within the structure.

**Q4: What are the consequences of living on an inauspicious plot?**

A: According to Vastu principles, living on an inauspicious plot without proper remediation may lead to financial difficulties, health problems, family discord, and obstacles to professional growth. Different plot irregularities correlate with specific challenges – for example, triangular plots may bring legal issues, while plots cut in the northeast may affect prosperity and mental peace.

**Q5: How much deviation from perfect proportions is acceptable in Vastu?**

A: Vastu texts suggest that a land tilt of up to 20 degrees (off square by 20 degrees) is acceptable. For rectangular plots, a length-to-width ratio of up to 1:2 is considered auspicious. Beyond these parameters, remedial measures become increasingly important to counterbalance potential negative energies.
