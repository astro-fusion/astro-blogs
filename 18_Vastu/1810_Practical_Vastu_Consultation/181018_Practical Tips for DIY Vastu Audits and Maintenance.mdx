## Key Principles and Applications {#key-principles}

### Principle 1: Integration of DIY Audit Routines
**Description**: Integrating self-audits and maintenance as an ongoing practice ensures consistent Vastu harmony.

**Practical Application**:
- Set a regular schedule for entrance cleaning, twice weekly if possible
- Create a checklist covering each key area: entrance, living room, bedrooms, kitchen, and bathrooms
- Use natural cleaners like salt, lemon, or vinegar for purification[9]
- Verify directional alignments and furniture placements as per Vastu

### Principle 2: Clutter Management and Decluttering
**Description**: Clutter disrupts both the flow of energy and the auspiciousness of a home.

**Implementation Steps**:
1. Remove unnecessary items from entrances, hallways, and living spaces
2. Allocate a dedicated decluttering day each month
3. Use storage solutions that keep surfaces tidy and promote order
4. After decluttering, clean and refresh spaces to reinvigorate energies[6][9]

### Principle 3: Periodic Review of Room Functions and Decor
**Description**: Room purposes and decor should evolve with the household’s needs and Vastu guidelines.

**Expert Recommendations**:
- Quarterly, review the use of each room to ensure it matches Vastu best practices (e.g., bedrooms in southwest, puja rooms in northeast)
- Update decor to maintain positivity: use calming landscapes, avoid heavy or dark artwork in the north and east[7][10]
- Adjust color schemes as needed: prefer light colors for peace and abundance
- Remove or reposition electronics and heavy furniture to prevent blocked energy[7]

## Calculation and Analysis Methods {#calculation}

### Step-by-Step Calculation Process

**Required Information**:
- Home’s directional orientation (use a compass app)
- List of rooms and main functions
- Inventory of decor and electronics

**Calculation Formula**:

1. **Entrance Audit**: Is the main door aligned with north, east, or northeast? Is it free of clutter, dirt, and obstacles? Is there a threshold and clean doormat present?[6]
2. **Clutter Score**: Count visible items without a practical or aesthetic purpose in each room. Aim for less than 5 per room.
3. **Energy Flow Check**: Walk through and note any blocked pathways, dark corners, or poorly ventilated areas.
4. **Decor Audit**: List current wall art and decorative objects. Are they in recommended directions? Are color schemes light and harmonious?

Regularly score each area and set improvement goals for the next audit.

## Practical Vastu Applications {#practical-applications}

### Entrance Cleaning Checklist
- Sweep and mop the area near the main door every other day[6][9]
- Wipe the door and threshold with salt water or lemon water weekly
- Keep footwear, garbage bins, and unused items away from the entrance
- Ensure the doormat is clean and replaced regularly[6][9]
- Add auspicious symbols or plants according to Vastu

### Clutter Management Routine
- Set aside a decluttering session each month for every major room
- Remove broken objects, expired items, and anything unused for six months
- Use storage baskets or cabinets to keep visible surfaces clear[9]
- Donate or repurpose items with sentimental energy but no daily use

### Periodic Review of Room Functions and Decor
- Every quarter, assess whether room usage aligns with Vastu zones (e.g., avoid placing bedrooms in northeast, kitchens in southeast)
- Refresh wall art and decor: opt for soft nature scenes, calming colors[7][10]
- Invite natural light and fresh air by cleaning window panes and opening windows
- Confirm that electronics and heavy items don’t block main pathways or energy flow[7]

## Common Mistakes to Avoid {#common-mistakes}

- Neglecting the entrance and accumulating shoes or clutter there
- Using harsh chemicals or neglecting regular cleaning routines, which can invite negative energy
- Ignoring the importance of regular audits; Vastu is not a “set it and forget it” practice
- Overloading living spaces with heavy or dark decor, especially in north and east directions[7][10]
- Placing mirrors directly opposite the entrance or cluttering thresholds[6]

**Q1: How often should I conduct a DIY Vastu audit at home?**
