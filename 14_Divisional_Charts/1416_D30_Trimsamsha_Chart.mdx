## What is D30 Trimsamsha Chart? {#what-is-title}

**D30 Trimsamsha Chart** is a specialized divisional chart in Vedic astrology, also known as the Trimsamsa or D-30 chart. It divides each sign into thirty unequal parts, with each part measuring exactly 1 degree. The chart is primarily used to assess the misfortunes, health mishaps, internal weaknesses, and lingering karmic patterns—especially those originating from past lives—that a native might encounter in their lifetime[4][5][1].

**Key aspects:**
- **Astrological Significance**: The D30 Trimsamsha Chart highlights the subtle planetary influences that govern our encounters with adversity, enemies, and psychological wounds.  
- **Vastu Applications**: It can guide Vastu remedies and design by highlighting the directional energies susceptible to negativity or blockages.
- **Spiritual Dimensions**: The D30 chart reflects the subconscious mind and past life influences, showing the roots of obstacles at energetic and karmic levels[4][5][6].

### Historical Context
The concept of the D30 Trimsamsha Chart finds its origins in classical texts such as Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra, where ancient sages detailed the use of divisional charts to decode complex interactions between planetary energies and life experiences[2][7].
