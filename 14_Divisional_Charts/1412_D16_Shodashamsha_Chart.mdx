## What is D16 Shodashamsha Chart? {#what-is-title}

**The D16 Shodashamsha Chart** is one of the pivotal divisional charts in Vedic astrology, known for its focus on vehicles, comforts, and material enjoyments. Each zodiac sign is divided into sixteen equal segments, each called a Shodashamsha, representing 1°52'30” of the sign[2][8]. This chart is also called the Kalamsa chart and is primarily used to analyze an individual’s potential for acquiring movable assets such as vehicles, as well as their experience of luxuries and comforts in life[1][3][8].

- **Astrological significance**: Assesses the karmic patterns and possibilities related to vehicles and material luxuries.
- **Vastu applications**: Offers guidance for arranging homes or workspaces to align with comfort and vehicle-related energies.
- **Spiritual dimensions**: Reflects the soul’s journey through the enjoyment and acquisition of material comforts, revealing past-life influences[1][5].

### Historical Context

The concept of the D16 Shodashamsha Chart is rooted in the ancient Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra, where it is described as a means to examine the happiness and vehicles one may possess, as well as the broader implications of movable comforts on a person's life journey[1][5][6].

## Calculation and Analysis Methods {#calculation}

### Step-by-Step Calculation Process

**Required Information**:
- Accurate birth date, time, and place (essential for all Varga charts).
- Residence orientation and layout (for applied Vastu analysis).
- Areas of concern (e.g., car accidents, vehicle losses, or lack of comfort).

**Calculation Formula**:
- Each sign (30°) is divided into sixteen Shodashamshas of 1°52'30” each.
- The planetary positions are mapped to these segments to determine their specific influence on movable comforts[2][8].

**General Method**:
1. Cast the natal chart (D1), then generate the D16 chart using astrology software or manual division[3][8].
2. Identify the D16 Lagna and key houses (4th for vehicles, 6th and 11th for gains/losses).
3. Analyze the strength and dignity of Venus, Moon, Mars, and the ascendant lord in the D16 chart.
4. Evaluate planetary aspects, conjunctions, and dashas for timing specific events.
